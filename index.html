Digitize the Data

When you want to share your data with others, the last thing that you want to do is make copies and mail a physical envelope. But most information is still printed, or even handwritten, and passed around on paper!

If you have a dataset which would be useful to open up, and share with citizens and developers, the first most important thing that you can do is to find the original file or make a digital copy.

Is it good to scan in some documents? Yes and no. If there is a digital copy, try to find the original file so it will have the top quality and be easily readable by people and computer programs.

Publish Open Data

Upload files to your website, CKAN, or GitHub. For your first open data project, it isn't so important where you put the files, as long as they are public and findable.

As soon as you have some data online, you won't be getting as many requests for copies. But people might ask about new datasets!

Make a plan to keep these files updated over time. If you are uploading the 2015 budget, make a plan for someone to upload the 2016 budget next year.
